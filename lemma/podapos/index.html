Dictionary (LSJ)

ποδαπός, ή, όν, A. from what country?: hence, generally, whence? where born? Hdt. 7.218 (as v.l.), A. Ch. 575, S. OC 1160, E. Cyc. 276, etc.; τίς καὶ π.; Pl. Ap. 20b; π. τὸ γένος; Ar. Pax 186, cf. Av. 108; of wine, π. ὁ Βρόμιος; Θάσιος Alex. 230. 2. generally, of what sort? π. τὸ δῶρον; S. Fr. 453: freq. in later Gr., Ev.Matt. 8.27, Ev.Luc. 1.29, Hermog. Inv. 1.1, etc.; in a play on both senses, π. τὸ γένος; πλούσιος Alex. 90; π. (sc. κύων) ; οἷος . . μὴ δάκνειν D. 25.40. Adv. -πῶς Hdn. Gr.2.925; ποταπῶς Gloss. (Phryn. 39 considers signf. 2 un-Attic, but S.l.c. is cited by Sch. D.T.p.239 H.: the spelling ποταπός (wh. is f.l. in Alex. 230 (codd. Ath.)) is found in later Gr., ll. cc. supr., cf. Phld. Vit.p.25J., D.H. 7.57, Ph. 1.157(v.l.), Philostr. VA 3.16, Luc. Par. 22, Jul. Or. 4.138c. ποδ- = Lat. quod; for the termination cf. ἀλλοδαπός (and v. A.D. Synt. 20.15). Derived fr. ποίου δαπέδου by Phryn.l.c., Phot.)
