Dictionary (LSJ)

τριάκοντα [ᾱκ], Ep. and Ion. τρῐήκ-, late Gr. τρῐάντα (q. v.), οἱ, αἱ, τά, indecl.; but gen. τριηκόντων Hes. Op. 696, also in later Ep., Call. Fr. 67; dat. τριηκόντεσσιν AP 11.41 (Phld.):—A. thirty, Il. 2.516, IG 12.1.9, etc.; τ. καὶ ἕνα thirty-one, ib.42(1).71.9 (Epid., iii B. C.). II. οἱ τ., esp., 1. at Sparta, a council of war of thirty, X. Ages. 1.7, HG 3.4.2,20, 4.1.5, al. 2. at Athens, the Thirty, commonly called the thirty tyrants, appointed on the taking of Athens (404 B.C.), ib.2.3.2, IG 22.6.12, Pl. Ap. 32c, Arist. Rh. 1400a18. 3. certain magistrates, v. τεσσαράκοντα; οἱ λογισταὶ οἱ τ. (IG 12.91.8) are sts. called simply οἱ τ., IG 12.191.1, 193.1. [In late Epigr. ᾰκ, AP 11.298, etc.]
