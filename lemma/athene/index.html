Dictionary (LSJ)

Ἀθήνη, ἡ, A. Athene, Il. 1.194, etc.; Παλλὰς Ἀ. ib.400, etc.:—also Ἀθηναίη, Παλλὰς Ἀ. ib.221,200, etc.:—Att. Ἀθηναία, A. Eu. 288, Ar. Eq. 763, Pax 271; Ἀ. Πολιάς Av. 828, cf. X. An. 7.3.39, and earlier Attic Inscrr.: contr. Ἀθηνᾶ, which in cent. iv superseded the fuller form: Dor. Ἀθάνα (this form and Ἀθηναία are the only ones used in Trag.); Ἀθαναία IG 1.373105, Theoc. 15.80: Aeol. Ἀθανάα [νᾰ], Alc. 9, Theoc. 28.1 (also in some Attic Inscrr., as IG 1.351; Ἀθηνάα ib.373120). 2. Ἀθηνᾶς ψῆφος casting vote, from that of A. given for Orestes, Philostr. VS 2.3. 3. = Ἀθῆναι, q.v. 4. Pythag. name for 7 (cf. ἀειπάρθενος), TheoSm.p.103 H. 5. name of a plaster, Orib. Fr. 88.
